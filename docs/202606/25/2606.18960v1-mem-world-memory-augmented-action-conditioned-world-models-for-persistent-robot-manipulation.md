## 摘要
动作条件世界模型已成为机器人学习的一种有前途的范式，通过生成动作一致的视频展开，为昂贵的真实世界实验提供了可扩展的替代方案。然而，在操作中，持久世界建模仍然具有挑战性：频繁的末端执行器遮挡和快速的腕部相机运动使得当前观察不足以预测未来视图，导致模型忘记或幻觉在早期帧中看到的场景细节。现有的内存检索策略通常无法在动态操作场景中识别信息丰富的历史。为解决这一限制，我们提出了Mem-World，一个内存增强的多视图动作条件世界模型。其核心是W-VMem，一个以腕部视图为中心的4D面元索引内存，将历史观察锚定到时间演化的表面元素。通过显式建模场景元素被观察的时间和位置，W-VMem能够基于未来动作实现几何感知的相关历史帧检索。在生成过程中，通过基于面元的渲染和评分选择相关历史帧，为预测提供信息丰富且非冗余的上下文。大量实验表明，Mem-World在复杂操作场景中生成持久展开，比Ctrl-World实现更可靠的策略评估，与真实世界性能的皮尔逊相关性提高了14.5%，并通过合成数据生成支持有效的策略改进，将长时域任务的成功率从58%提高到72%。

## Abstract
Action-conditioned world models have emerged as a promising paradigm for robot learning, offering a scalable alternative to costly real-world experimentation by generating action-consistent video rollouts. However, persistent world modeling remains challenging in manipulation: frequent end-effector occlusions and rapid wrist-camera motion make the current observation insufficient for predicting future views, causing models to forget or hallucinate scene details seen in earlier frames. Existing memory retrieval strategies often fail to identify informative history in dynamic manipulation scenarios. To address this limitation, we propose Mem-World, a memory-augmented multi-view action-conditioned world model. At its core, we present W-VMem, a 4D wrist-view-centered surfel-indexed memory that anchors historical observations to temporally evolving surface elements. By explicitly modeling when and where scene elements are observed, W-VMem enables geometry-aware retrieval of relevant history frames conditioned on future actions. During generation, relevant history frames are selected via surfel-based rendering and scoring, providing informative and non-redundant context for prediction. Extensive experiments show that Mem-World generates persistent rollouts in complex manipulation scenarios, enables more reliable policy evaluation than Ctrl-World, improving the Pearson correlation with real-world performance by 14.5\%, and supports effective policy improvement through synthetic data generation, increasing success rates from 58\% to 72\% on long-horizon tasks.
